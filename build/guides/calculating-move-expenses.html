Calculating Move Expenses

A guide containing things to consider when counting the cost of the move itself.

Elijah Watson | September 30, 2019

An essential part of any moving out plan is the actual moving process. There are many things to consider. Some of the most important things include the moving equipment, a moving vehicle, moving help, and temporary storage. Planning for these things will help make the move go much smoother.

Moving equipment varies significantly based on circumstances. Boxes are almost always necessary. Most moving companies sell all sorts of sizes and types to perfectly fit your needs. Using boxes you already have can potentially reduce this cost. Straps are also helpful if you are trying to hold things in place. Depending on the vehicle you are using, they may even be necessary for safety. A dolly can also significantly ease the difficulty of moving heavy objects around. Again, these can be found at most moving stores, and you could also check with friends and family to see if they have one available.

The next step is to select a moving vehicle. Ideally, you could simply use a truck and trailer that you, a friend, or a family member has. It may take a few trips, but it is the most cost efficient option and is typically less stressful since you do not have to try and fit all your stuff into one truckload. Another option is a moving truck. Though more expensive, it may be necessary if you have lots of large furniture you are trying to move and/or you do not have easy access to a truck and trailer. Be sure and research different options. Picking the right type of truck, the right package, and the right company can help keep the cost down.

In most cases, moving help will not be a big issue. Friends and family can help with packing, loading, unloading, and unpacking. However, if you are moving long distance or do not have friends and family available to help with the move, hiring some moving help may be necessary. In many areas, there are companies that specialize in this type of help. Other times, it may be necessary to think outside the box when looking for help. Temporary storage will only be necessary in certain situations. Do you have a lot of stuff? Are you are in a transition period where you need to move out but are not going to be immediately moving in to a long-term residence? Temporary storage might be a good option. There are outdoor and indoor options depending on what you are trying to store. Take time to consider your needs, then research different choices. Size, location, security, services, a price should all be considered.
