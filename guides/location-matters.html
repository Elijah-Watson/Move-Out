Location Matters

An importnat part of any move out plan is understanding the impact your location will have.

Elijah Watson | September 23, 2019

When moving out, an important consideration is where you are moving. Perhaps you already know exactly where you want to go, or maybe you havenâ€™t quite decided yet. Regardless, putting some thought into what all the location you are moving to affects will help you to make better decisions and get a better idea of what your future expenses might be.

Housing. If you choose to live in a rural or suburban area, the most reasonable housing may be a rental home, duplex, or trailer whereas an apartment might make more since in the city. Additionally, the cost of housing can vary wildly depending on the city or state. For example, housing in New York City can easily be double or triple a comparable option in other cities.

Transportation. Depending on the area you live, good public transportation may or may not be available. If it is, you must weigh the pros and cons of each form of transportation (specifically cost and convenience) and decide which option fits your circumstance the best. If it is not, a vehicle will almost definitely be a necessity.

Taxes. Taxes are something that few people fully understand. Even with tools to help, it can be a headache to deal with and a cause for anxiety. However, it is worth looking into the taxes you may face in an area you are considering moving to because they can make a significant impact on your finances. The three main categories that taxes fall into are taxes on income, taxes on property, and taxes on goods and services. The implementations and amounts can vary wildly. For example, Tennessee has a low property tax, high sales tax, and no income tax while Oregon has a moderate property tax, high income tax, and no sales tax.

Cost of living. The average cost of items in general can vary between areas. Things such as gasoline, groceries, internet, utilities, and more can all change based on location. For example, the cost of groceries in New York City can be up to 30% higher than other cities.

Location definitely plays a major role in what your budget will look like after you move. It is important to consider these factors whether you are still choosing a location or trying to create a financial plan. By taking the time to research these things, however, you will set yourself up for success as you make your move.
